Land banking

Land Banking

Land banking is the practice of buying undeveloped land in anticipation of its future appreciation in value, typically driven by urbanization, infrastructure development, and population growth. Investors acquire land at a low price and hold it long-term, with the goal of selling it for a profit once its value increases, or they may choose to develop it later. It is often seen as a long-term, strategic investment that capitalizes on the finite supply of land.

How it works

Purchase:Acquire land in strategically chosen areas that are currently underdeveloped but show potential for future growth.
Hold: Hold the land for a long period, often years, during which its value is expected to rise due to factors like new infrastructure (roads, airports) and city expansion.
Profit: Sell the land at a higher price after its value has appreciated, or develop it for commercial or residential purposes.
Why it is attractive
Long-term growth: It's a strategy that can lead to significant returns
